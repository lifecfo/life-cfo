# Keystone — Core Experience & Page Architecture Plan
_Last updated: 2026-01-21_

## Keystone’s North Star

A person closes the app feeling lighter, because a decision that used to loop in their mind is now safely held — thoughtfully processed, wisely actioned, clearly recorded, and released.

Keystone is not a logging tool.
It is not a task manager.
It is a calm, values-anchored decision system that reduces cognitive load and builds trust over time.

---

## The Keystone Decision Lifecycle (Agreed Model)

This lifecycle is the **conceptual spine** of the product.  
All pages, flows, and AI behaviour must align to this.

1. **Capture**  
   Get decisions out of the mind.

2. **Thinking**  
   Make it safe to think.  
   AI does as much thinking as possible to reduce mental burden.

3. **Decisions**  
   Commit clearly.  
   Decisions are recorded and placed into context (a constellation).

4. **Revisit**  
   Bring decisions back *only* with intent and purpose.

5. **Chapters**  
   Honour and release decisions that no longer need active mental space.  
   Still available for learning and future reference.

The Engine runs in the background across all stages — invisible to the user.

---

## Primary Navigation Structure (Agreed)

These represent **psychological modes**, not features.

- **Home**
- **Capture**
- **Thinking**
- **Decisions**
- **Revisit**
- **Chapters**
- **Inputs** (Accounts / Bills / Income)

---

## Page-by-Page Purpose & Feel

### 1. Home (Landing Page)

**Role:** Psychological stabiliser  
**This page is not a dashboard.**

Home exists to:
- reduce cognitive load within seconds
- orient the user emotionally
- reassure them that nothing critical is being missed

#### What MUST be on Home

**A single primary input**
- “What’s on your mind?”
- Accepts statements, fragments, emotions
- Can be pure unload OR AI-assisted framing
- Always safe, never demanding

**AI Compass (Signals, not data)**
- AI synthesises all underlying data
- Shows calm conclusions, not numbers
- Examples:
  - “Everything looks stable right now.”
  - “One upcoming bill may need attention.”
  - “You’re safe to spend within your usual range.”
  - “There’s a decision here that could reduce stress later.”

These signals:
- are human
- are brief
- link out *only if the user wants to go deeper*

#### What must NOT be on Home

- Lists
- Backlogs
- Raw financial data
- “Notes” sections
- To-do style elements
- Multiple competing inputs

Home is a place to **arrive, unload, orient, and leave**.

---

### 2. Capture

**Role:** Externalise mental load

- Captured items may include:
  - text
  - photos
  - PDFs
  - links
- No decision pressure
- No required categorisation

AI may:
- auto-classify in the background
- suggest relevance or importance
- prepare context for Thinking

Capture is allowed to be messy.

---

### 3. Thinking

**Role:** Safe thinking space

This page answers:
> “What haven’t I finished thinking through yet?”

Key principles:
- No urgency
- No guilt
- No pressure to decide

AI’s role here:
- surface best-practice approaches
- ask clarifying questions
- provide pros / cons
- suggest angles the user may not have considered
- reduce emotional and cognitive burden

Draft decisions can stay here indefinitely.

---

### 4. Decisions

**Role:** Decision ledger (not a task list)

This is:
- a calm record of resolved choices
- a source of grounding and reassurance

Users come here to:
- remember what they’ve decided
- avoid re-deciding
- see the shape of their life choices

Most days:
- users should *not* need to do anything here

Key affordances:
- pin (long-term relevance)
- expand/collapse (memory jog)
- minimal action buttons

---

### 5. Revisit

**Role:** Intentional resurfacing

Decisions appear here only if:
- they have a review cadence
- they are due or due soon

This is not nagging.
This is trust-based resurfacing.

Typical flow:
- re-read context
- mark reviewed
- add a note
- adjust cadence
- clear review if no longer needed

Outcome:
- the mental loop is closed again

---

### 6. Chapters

**Role:** Honour and release

Chapters are not archives.
They are **emotionally closed loops**.

A Chapter represents:
- a season that has ended
- a decision set that no longer needs attention
- something the user is done carrying

Chapters:
- feel distinct from Decisions
- are visually quieter
- may contain AI-generated “closure summaries”
- remain available for learning and reference

This is where Keystone helps people *let go*.

---

### 7. Inputs (Accounts / Bills / Income)

**Role:** Truth layer

Inputs exist to:
- provide accurate data
- feed the Engine
- support AI conclusions

They are:
- not the emotional centre of the app
- not the landing experience
- not where decisions live

Most users should rarely need to open Inputs.

---

## The Compass Concept (Critical)

**Compass is not a page.  
Compass is the intelligence living on Home.**

Compass:
- synthesises data + decisions + cadence
- surfaces calm, human signals
- guides without instructing
- reassures without overwhelming

Users should *feel* the Compass, not manage it.

---

## Design Principles to Enforce Everywhere

- Reduce cognitive load within 5 seconds
- Never require action unless it truly matters
- Prefer conclusions over data
- Prefer trust over control
- Prefer calm over cleverness
- AI speaks sparingly, kindly, and only when helpful

---

## Open Questions (Intentionally Kept Open)

- How AI visually represents Compass signals (iteration pending)
- How Chapters are visually marked and entered
- How decision “constellations” are grouped and surfaced
- How personal importance (emotional weight vs severity) is refined over time

These are *design evolutions*, not blockers.

---

## Summary

Keystone is not helping people do more.
It is helping them carry less.

Every page exists to support that truth.

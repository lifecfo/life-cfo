# Life CFO — CFO Model

Last updated: 2026-03-11

## The role
Life CFO is the role the system plays for the user.

The system should behave like a calm, competent financial counterpart for a household:
- grounded in reality
- good at reducing uncertainty
- clear about trade-offs
- restrained in tone
- not attention-seeking
- not controlling

## What a good CFO does
A good CFO:
- makes complexity safer for other people
- reduces uncertainty before it becomes fear
- identifies what does not deserve attention
- preserves decision integrity over time
- surfaces trade-offs without forcing outcomes
- communicates calmly under pressure

That is the behavioral model for Life CFO.

## What this means in product terms

### 1. Stabilise first
Life CFO should first answer:
- Are things steady?
- What actually matters?
- What can safely wait?

It should not lead with optimisation.

### 2. Reduce unnecessary attention
A core function of Life CFO is deciding what does not need to be surfaced right now.

If everything is visible, the system is failing.

### 3. Preserve reasoning
When a household reaches a conclusion, Life CFO should help preserve:
- what was decided
- why it was decided
- what assumptions mattered
- what would justify revisiting it

### 4. Keep agency with the user
Life CFO may explain trade-offs and consequences.

It should not pressure the user or act like the final authority.

### 5. Operate in calm cycles
A strong CFO does not create constant noise.

The system should feel like:
- quiet most of the time
- analytical when asked
- explanatory when needed
- silent again afterward

## Product standard
If a feature feels loud, demanding, unclear, or manipulative, it is not behaving like a good CFO for a family.

## Anchor sentence
Life CFO should feel like something competent is already handling things — even if the user is not.

Life CFO
Data Retention and Disposal Policy
Effective Date: 5 March 2026
Organization: Life CFO
1. Purpose
This policy defines how Life CFO retains, manages, and deletes user data in order to protect personal information and comply with applicable data protection and privacy regulations.
2. Scope
This policy applies to all data collected, processed, or stored by Life CFO, including:
•	User account information
•	Financial data retrieved through Plaid
•	Application usage data
•	System logs required for security and reliability
3. Data Retention Principles
Life CFO retains user data only for as long as necessary to provide the service and maintain system integrity. Data retention follows these principles:
•	Data is collected only for legitimate application functionality.
•	Data retention periods are minimized where possible.
•	Users retain control over their financial connections and account data.
4. Financial Data (Plaid)
If users choose to connect financial accounts:
•	Financial data such as account balances and transactions may be retrieved through Plaid.
•	Life CFO does not store bank login credentials.
•	Data retrieved from Plaid is used solely to provide application features.
Users may disconnect financial accounts at any time, which stops further data retrieval.
5. Account Deletion
Users may request deletion of their account and associated data.
Upon deletion:
•	Access credentials are removed.
•	Associated user records and linked financial data are deleted or anonymized where technically feasible.
•	Backup systems may retain encrypted copies temporarily for disaster recovery purposes but are automatically overwritten according to infrastructure retention schedules.
6. Security Logs
Limited system logs may be retained for security monitoring and operational reliability. These logs are retained only as long as necessary for troubleshooting, auditing, or abuse prevention.
7. Infrastructure Providers
Life CFO uses trusted infrastructure providers to host application systems and databases. These providers may store encrypted backups as part of their standard infrastructure security practices.
8. Data Disposal
Data is disposed of using secure deletion practices provided by the hosting infrastructure. Access to stored data is restricted using role-based permissions and household-scoped access controls.
9. Policy Review
This policy is reviewed periodically and updated as the Life CFO platform evolves to ensure ongoing compliance with privacy and data protection requirements.

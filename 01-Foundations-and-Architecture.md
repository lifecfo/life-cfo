**Keystone — Foundations \& Architecture (v1)**



**Status**



**Canonical — Locked unless a contradiction or safety issue is discovered**



1\. Purpose



Keystone exists to reduce cognitive load by coordinating decision-critical domains of life (starting with money and time), so humans do not have to mentally integrate them alone.



Keystone does not manage life.

It coordinates systems so humans can make fewer, better decisions, at the right moments, without pressure, guilt, or constant checking.



Success is measured by:



fewer missed obligations



fewer reactive decisions



less mental overhead



long periods of silence



If Keystone is doing its job well, it is rarely noticed.



2\. Core Philosophy (Non-Negotiable)

2.1 Human Authority



The human is always the final authority.



Keystone never assumes consent.



All actions require explicit permission at the correct scope.



Decline is a terminal, respected state.



2.2 Silence as Success



Keystone speaks only when human authority is required.



There are no feeds, dashboards, or “awareness” notifications by default.



Most of the time, the interface should be empty.



2.3 Restraint Over Intelligence Theatre



Keystone prioritises correctness, safety, and reliability over cleverness.



No explanations unless required for consent.



No coaching, persuasion, or optimisation framing.



2.4 Structural Help, Not Behavioural Control



Keystone reduces load by removing future decisions, not by shaping behaviour.



Constraints are structural (automation, routing, buffers), not psychological.



3\. What Keystone Explicitly Does NOT Do



Keystone will not:



give financial advice



recommend products or investments



optimise returns



track habits



score behaviour



gamify progress



maximise engagement



operate silently beyond granted consent



These exclusions are intentional and protective.



4\. Core Systems (v1 Scope)



Keystone v1 consists of three load-bearing systems:



4.1 Money Engine



Coordinates:



income detection



recurring bills



annual/irregular obligations



buffers and savings



bounded discretionary spending



Supports three best-practice money models:



Balanced (default)



Savings-First



Cash-Flow-First



All enforcement is structural and automated once approved.



4.2 Time Engine



Coordinates:



calendar data and/or manual weekly timetable



realistic capacity



protected time



overload prevention



Keystone does not optimise productivity or schedule tasks.

It prevents silent overload and poorly timed decisions.



4.3 Decision Inbox



The only surface where Keystone speaks.



Rules:



one item at a time



only when human authority is required



clear payoff



one primary action



return to silence after resolution



The Inbox is not a feed.

If it feels busy, the system is failing.



5\. Consent Model



All authority is mediated through explicit consent.



Consent Types



Action consent — one-time execution



Scope consent — repeatable actions within bounds



Structural consent — changes to system behaviour or flows



Re-consent is required when:



assumptions change



bounds are exceeded



new obligations enter scope



income cadence materially changes



No silent expansion is allowed.



6\. Declines \& Auditability



Declined decisions are respected immediately.



Declines are recorded and never resurfaced automatically.



Users may review and re-approve declined items intentionally.



Every meaningful action is written to an append-only audit log:



consent events



automations scheduled/executed



failures



pauses



overrides



Nothing is deleted or rewritten.



7\. Technical Posture (Bank-Grade Discipline)



Keystone is built with bank-grade discipline from day one:



strict data isolation per household



row-level security (RLS)



encryption at rest and in transit



idempotent automation execution



append-only audit logs



provider-agnostic integration boundaries



background jobs with retry and failure handling



no business logic in the client



This is a real company architecture, not a prototype stack.



8\. Regulatory Positioning (Intentional)



Keystone is:



coordination software



automation with consent



mechanical execution



Keystone is not:



a financial advisor



a bank



an investment platform



This positioning is deliberate to minimise regulatory burden while maximising safety and trust.



9\. Expansion Philosophy



Keystone is designed as a house:



Foundations are non-negotiable.



Load-bearing systems must not be altered casually.



New domains (health, education, relationships) may be added only if:



they reduce mental load



they respect silence



they fit the consent model



they do not multiply surface area unnecessarily



Depth is added layer by layer, not all at once.



10\. Definition of “Done” (v1)



Keystone v1 is successful when:



real households rely on it quietly



money obligations are handled without thought



time pressure is reduced



the Inbox is empty most days



trust is never violated



nothing feels clever — only calm
